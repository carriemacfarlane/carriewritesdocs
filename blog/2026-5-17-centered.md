I often see centered paragraphs when I proofread content for colleagues outside of Documentation. I suggest the text should be left-aligned, but when it goes out, it’s usually still centered.

The documentation style guide doesn’t apply to non-technical materials like website copy and online tutorials. So when I give feedback on formatting to these writers, I really need to motivate them to want to make the change. I need a new argument for left-aligned paragraph text.

What I’ve said in the past is that left-aligned text is accessible and readable for everyone. With left-aligned text, it’s easier to find the beginning of each new line. I’ve cited a few sources, but they're not getting through!

People center paragraphs because they feel this makes the content look designed, or curated. It’s symmetrical! I get that. Most of the content I consume is left-aligned, so center-aligned text definitely stands out to me too.

I searched for a different approach and I found a good one in marketing literature. Center-aligned paragraphs stand out for the wrong reasons. They seem unusual because **the big brands no longer use them**. Centered paragraphs are harder to read. Our message is stronger without them.

### What I’m Working On

* Proofreading external communications content

### Question

* How can I convince writers outside the Documentation Team that paragraphs of text shouldn’t be centered?

### Answer

* Contextualize our formatting decisions: The big brands know that center-aligned paragraphs are hard to read.
